About

I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science at the University of Arizona, and member of Security, Privacy and Reliability Lab . My research focuses on secure and trustworthy artificial intelligence, autonomous agents, and multi-agent systems.

My background spans intelligence analysis, cyber operations, operations research, data science, and electronic warfare analysis.

I study how AI-enabled systems behave in complex and adversarial environments, with the goal of translating rigorous technical research into secure, reliable, and operationally relevant capabilities.

Publications

An Evaluation of Chat Safety Moderations in Roblox

Priya Kaushik, Sonja Brown, Rakibul Hasan, and Sazzadur Rahaman

Under review at the 33rd ACM Conference on Computer and Communications Security (CCS).

We introduce a non-intrusive, OCR-based framework for collecting chat data from gameplay recordings, enabling empirical audits of moderation systems when chat data is not directly available in textual form. Using this framework, we collected approximately two million messages from 105,214 users and developed an LLM-assisted qualitative analysis approach to identify harmful content that escaped moderation and the strategies users employed to evade filtering.

Projects

Agentic Cyber

An experimental framework for studying the security and reliability of AI coding agents performing autonomous software-maintenance tasks. The project evaluates how model choice, execution strategy, iteration, and testing affect the correctness and security of agent-generated code.

Military Logistics Optimization

Developed and evaluated metaheuristic approaches to the Multi-Vehicle Routing Problem with Time Windows for military distribution networks. The study compared genetic algorithms, particle swarm optimization, and simulated annealing across repeated experiments and examined their performance, scalability, and suitability for operational decision support.
